Computer Science

Computing in the modern era carries the stereotype of being a male-dominated field, by natural talent; in fact, it was only when computers became viewed as more than office machines that computing became men’s work. With the past contributions of women (at a time of equal involvement) being of such significance, girls and women should be given more opportunities to engage in the same way, since much of their potential is currently going into waste. Further involvement of women should be encouraged as it would bring in more innovation crucial to further advancements as a joint effort of humanity. Julie A. James of Swinburne University of Technology argues that there is evidence to suggest that a typically feminine approach to management in this desired new environment may prove highly successful. From technical, philosophical and sociological standpoints, women’s involvement is undeniably crucial to the development of computing.

Girls, here are some reasons you should consider computer science:

  • CS in history: there were more opportunities for the female "computers" post-war, compared to the average women who returned to the domestic sphere
  • You get to do fulfilling work that is constantly influencing the world
  • High wage: the average computer science graduate makes over £30,000/year, compared to the national average of around £24,000
etc.

Scratch

My very first program was an animation made with scratch. Before doing A-level computer Science, I found scratch to be very helpful with learning underlying computer science logic. The turtle system is a post-scratch bridging system that supports code, with immediate visual feedback.

I'd like to encourage more girls to get into computing; I made this site with this aim and hope your visit had been inspiring. The scratch-like way of accessing biographies here demands no technical knowledge and was designed with the intention of lessening intimidation on reading into the subject. At the same time, the pseudocode “print” function in the interface would hopefully serve as a gateway to tech for beginners. This website was built from scratch by myself, with research from textbook resources to realise the technicality. I hope this artefact is a small step in the right direction for the better advancement of computing, shedding some light on the inspirational female figures.
